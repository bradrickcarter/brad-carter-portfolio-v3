## Selected Projects

### AllyIQ — AllyGPO | Lead Product Designer
**Domain:** Healthcare Inventory & Billing (Retina Practices)

**The problem:** Retina practices run on buy-and-bill — they purchase specialty drugs worth thousands of dollars per vial, administer them, and fight to get reimbursed. The tools most practices used were a mess of spreadsheets and disconnected systems.

**What was built:** A single platform (AllyIQ) that unified drug ordering, real-time inventory tracking, dispensing workflows, and billing reconciliation. Tracked the full financial lifecycle of every vial — from order to reimbursement — surfacing charge capture gaps and reconciliation errors before they became lost revenue.

**Team:** Led a team of 3 designers

**Outcome:** Staff went from juggling 3 tools to working in 1. Billing gaps closed.

**Why this matters for GoodDay:** This is almost exactly what GoodDay does — unifying operational, inventory, and financial workflows into a single system for operators who can't afford errors.

### Lab Results App — Baylor Scott & White | Lead Product Designer

### Heart Failure Care — Baylor Scott & White | Lead Product Designer
**Domain:** Patient-Facing Healthcare, Enrolled Program (MyBSWHealth app)

**The problem:** Hospital re-admittance in the first 90 days post-discharge is expensive and resource-intensive. Most heart failure patients go home with a diagnosis they don't fully understand and no feedback loop — no clear signal for when to escalate symptoms to their care team.

**What was built:** An enrolled patient companion inside the MyBSWHealth app for newly diagnosed heart failure patients. Features included:
- Daily weight tracking with 7-day trend graph and clinical alert thresholds (2+ lbs/day or 5+ lbs/week)
- Blood pressure tracking with status labels and persistent alerts for severely elevated/low readings
- Symptom self-check that triaged severity across three levels (green/yellow/red) and routed patients to the right level of care
- Plain-language education library (video) covering diet, medication, exercise, and symptom recognition
- Appointment notes with pre-populated suggestion prompts
- Medication financial assistance request flow

**Design principles:** Reduce cognitive load, design for clinical accuracy, plain language (not medical jargon), drive daily habit formation, always one tap from escalating to care team or 911.

**Pilot results:**
- 12 patients enrolled in first 90 days at pilot hospital
- 80% used tracking features (weight/blood pressure) daily
- 50% engaged with education content
- Zero hospital readmissions in the 90-day pilot window

**Why this matters:** Demonstrates clinical domain depth, complex workflow design, data-heavy UI with clinically validated thresholds, patient empathy, and real outcomes.

## Design Direction for the Portfolio Site

### Concept: The Living IDE
The approved design direction is a VS Code / IDE-inspired portfolio that visually communicates that Brad is both a designer and a builder. The layout is inspired by claude.com's homepage structure:

- Dark background (#0a0a0a)
- Left side: hero headline, summary, stats, CTAs
- Right side: a tabbed panel with two views:
  - **Builder tab** — code editor aesthetic with syntax-highlighted JS showing Brad's data as object properties
  - **Designer tab** — clean, designed card layout with the same information presented visually

### Visual style
- Dark, minimal, sophisticated
- Font: system sans-serif for UI, JetBrains Mono for code
- Accent color: #d4a96a (warm amber/gold dot in eyebrow)
- Code syntax colors: standard One Dark theme palette
- No gradients, no purple, no generic AI-portfolio aesthetics
- Max content width: ~1440px centered

### Typography reference
- **Source:** https://practicaltypography.com/ (Butterick's Practical Typography)
- Body text: 15–25px on screen; site uses 16px ✓
- Line height: 120–145% of font size; site uses 1.6 (160% — slightly above, acceptable for dark UI) ✓
- Line length: 45–90 characters; site caps at 65ch ✓
- Bold and italic are mutually exclusive — never combine ✓
- On dark backgrounds, avoid pure white text — use off-white (~#e8e8e8) to reduce harsh contrast from emissive screens
- Small labels (12px) need higher-contrast colors to remain legible — minimum #888 on dark backgrounds
- Color on non-interactive text should be used sparingly; reserve color emphasis for links and key actions
